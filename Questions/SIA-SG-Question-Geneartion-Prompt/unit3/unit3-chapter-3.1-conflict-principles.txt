TOPIC 3.1.1: WHAT IS CONFLICT?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
- Disagreement or opposition between people
- Can range from verbal dispute to physical violence
- Natural part of human interaction
- Can be managed and resolved

Types of Conflict:

VERBAL CONFLICT:
- Raised voices, arguments
- Insults, threats
- Complaints, disputes
- Most common type faced by security

PHYSICAL CONFLICT:
- Pushing, shoving
- Punches, kicks
- Use of weapons
- Most serious - avoid if possible

PASSIVE CONFLICT:
- Non-cooperation
- Deliberate obstruction
- Silent treatment
- Refusal to comply

Conflict in Security Officer Role:
- Shoplifters challenged in retail
- Visitors refused entry to corporate premises
- Trespassers asked to leave industrial sites
- Disputes between staff members
- Customer complaints
- Contractors not following procedures

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.2: CAUSES OF CONFLICT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Common Causes:

FRUSTRATION:
- Waiting too long
- Being refused something
- Unable to achieve goal
- Feeling ignored

ALCOHOL AND DRUGS:
- Impaired judgment
- Lowered inhibitions
- Increased aggression
- Unpredictable behaviour

PERSONAL ISSUES:
- Stress from outside situation
- Relationship problems
- Financial worries
- Mental health issues

FEELING DISRESPECTED:
- Perceived rudeness
- Loss of face (especially in public)
- Being talked down to
- Discrimination

MISUNDERSTANDING:
- Poor communication
- Language barriers
- Unclear instructions
- Different expectations

TERRITORIAL:
- Perceived invasion of space
- Property disputes
- "This is my area"

GROUP DYNAMICS:
- Showing off to peers
- Group mentality
- Peer pressure
- Competition

Environmental Factors:
- Overcrowding
- Heat/poor ventilation
- Long queues
- Uncomfortable conditions
- Noise

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.3: THE CONFLICT ESCALATION MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Stages of Escalation:

STAGE 1 - CALM:
- Normal behaviour
- Rational thinking
- Open to communication
- Best time to address issues

STAGE 2 - TRIGGER:
- Something happens
- Mood changes
- Frustration begins
- Early warning signs

STAGE 3 - ESCALATION:
- Emotion increasing
- Voice raised
- Body language changes
- Rational thinking decreasing

STAGE 4 - CRISIS:
- Peak of emotion
- Potential for violence
- Not thinking rationally
- Fight or flight response

STAGE 5 - RECOVERY:
- Emotion subsiding
- Beginning to calm
- May be exhausted
- Still vulnerable to re-escalation

STAGE 6 - POST-CRISIS:
- Return to calm
- Reflection
- May feel embarrassed
- Opportunity for resolution

Key Principle:
- Intervene EARLY - easier to manage
- Each stage harder to de-escalate
- Prevention better than cure
- Know the warning signs

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.4: HUMAN RESPONSES TO CONFLICT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Fight, Flight, Freeze, Posture (FFFP):

FIGHT:
- Aggressive response
- Confrontational
- May become physical
- Adrenaline-driven

FLIGHT:
- Escape response
- Run away from conflict
- Avoid confrontation
- Self-preservation

FREEZE:
- Unable to respond
- Paralysis
- Mind goes blank
- Common under extreme stress

POSTURE:
- Making themselves look bigger
- Puffing chest
- Loud voice
- Intimidation without actual violence

Physiological Effects of Stress:
- Increased heart rate
- Rapid breathing
- Tunnel vision
- Auditory exclusion
- Fine motor skill loss
- Adrenaline surge
- Dry mouth
- Sweating

Impact on Security Officers:
- May experience same responses
- Training helps manage
- Awareness is key
- Breathing techniques help

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.5: THE ASK MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Components:

ATTITUDE:
- Professional approach
- Calm demeanour
- Confident but not arrogant
- Respectful
- Willing to help
- Non-judgmental
- Empathetic

SKILLS:
- Communication skills
- Observation
- Active listening
- De-escalation techniques
- Problem-solving
- Body language awareness
- Conflict resolution

KNOWLEDGE:
- Understanding human behaviour
- Legal powers and limitations
- Company policies
- Escalation procedures
- When to call for help
- Site-specific information

Importance of ASK:
- All three needed together
- Attitude sets the tone
- Skills provide the tools
- Knowledge guides decisions
- Weakness in one affects others

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.6: WARNING SIGNS OF CONFLICT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Verbal Warning Signs:
- Raised voice
- Swearing/insults
- Threats
- Short, abrupt answers
- Refusal to communicate
- Sarcasm
- Personal comments

Non-Verbal Warning Signs:
- Clenched fists
- Pointing finger
- Invasion of personal space
- Pacing/restlessness
- Staring/fixed gaze
- Red face/neck
- Jaw clenching
- Rapid breathing
- Removal of jacket/glasses

Behavioural Warning Signs:
- Ignoring instructions
- Sudden silence
- Looking around (checking for witnesses/escape)
- Positioning for advantage
- Target glance (looking at chin/throat)
- Thousand-yard stare

Importance of Recognition:
- Early warning = early intervention
- Gives time to prepare
- Opportunity to de-escalate
- Safer for everyone

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.7: POSITIVE AND NEGATIVE APPROACHES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Positive Approaches:

STAYING CALM:
- Controlled voice
- Measured responses
- Professional demeanour
- Not taking it personally

SHOWING RESPECT:
- Treating person as individual
- Listening to their point
- Acknowledging their feelings
- Using their name appropriately

FINDING SOLUTIONS:
- Offering alternatives
- Looking for compromise
- Problem-solving approach
- Win-win if possible

APPROPRIATE LANGUAGE:
- Polite but firm
- Clear and simple
- Non-provocative
- Offering choices

Negative Approaches (to avoid):

AGGRESSIVE BEHAVIOUR:
- Shouting back
- Threatening
- Invading space
- Physical gestures

DISMISSIVE ATTITUDE:
- Ignoring concerns
- Belittling person
- Not listening
- "It's not my problem"

PROVOCATIVE LANGUAGE:
- Insults
- Sarcasm
- Blame language
- "Calm down" (often escalates)

INCONSISTENCY:
- Different rules for different people
- Backing down then enforcing
- Unclear expectations
- Contradicting colleagues

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.1.8: ROLE OF SECURITY OFFICER IN CONFLICT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Role:
- Prevent conflict where possible
- De-escalate when conflict arises
- Protect people and property
- Resolve situations professionally
- Know when to call for help

Responsibilities:
- Remain professional at all times
- Not become part of the problem
- Use minimum intervention necessary
- Document incidents
- Report to appropriate persons
- Learn from incidents

Limitations:
- Not there to "win" arguments
- Cannot force compliance (usually)
- Must work within the law
- Must follow company policy
- Physical intervention last resort

When to Withdraw:
- Personal safety at risk
- Situation beyond capability
- Waiting for backup
- Tactical advantage in withdrawing
- No shame in retreat
