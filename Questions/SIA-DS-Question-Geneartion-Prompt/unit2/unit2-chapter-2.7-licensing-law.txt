TOPIC 2.7.1: LICENSING ACT 2003 - OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose:
- Primary legislation for licensed premises in England and Wales
- Unified previous fragmented licensing regimes
- Single framework for alcohol, entertainment, late-night refreshment
- Administered by local authority licensing committees

What It Covers:
- Sale and supply of alcohol
- Regulated entertainment
- Late-night refreshment (hot food/drink 11pm-5am)
- Premises licences
- Personal licences
- Temporary events

Key Principle:
- Promotion of the four licensing objectives
- All decisions must consider these objectives
- Licence conditions support the objectives

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.2: THE FOUR LICENSING OBJECTIVES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

These are FUNDAMENTAL - know them:

1. PREVENTION OF CRIME AND DISORDER
- Reducing crime on and around premises
- Preventing violence and antisocial behaviour
- Drug dealing prevention
- Door supervisors key to this objective

2. PUBLIC SAFETY
- Safety of persons on premises
- Fire safety
- Capacity limits
- First aid provision
- Safe environment

3. PREVENTION OF PUBLIC NUISANCE
- Noise control
- Light pollution
- Disturbance to residents
- Litter
- Behaviour of customers leaving

4. PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM HARM
- Preventing under-age drinking
- Preventing under-age entry where inappropriate
- Child safeguarding
- Age verification

Door Supervisor Role:
- Support ALL four objectives
- Through actions and decisions
- Searching, monitoring, ejecting
- Age verification
- Capacity management

Memory Aid: "CPPP" - Crime, Public safety, Public nuisance, Protect children

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.3: TYPES OF LICENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PREMISES LICENCE:
- Authorises specific premises for licensable activities
- Applied for by premises owner/operator
- Subject to conditions
- Displayed on premises
- Specifies hours of operation
- Specifies permitted activities
- Can be reviewed by interested parties
- Can have conditions added, varied, or revoked

PERSONAL LICENCE:
- Held by individual person
- Authorises selling/supplying alcohol
- Required for at least one person to authorise sales
- Valid for life (since 2015, previously 10 years)
- Can be revoked for relevant offences
- Holder can work at any licensed premises

CLUB PREMISES CERTIFICATE:
- For qualifying members' clubs
- Similar to premises licence
- Different application process
- For genuine members' clubs

TEMPORARY EVENT NOTICE (TEN):
- For one-off events
- Maximum 499 people at any one time
- Maximum 168 hours duration (7 days)
- Limits on number per year per premises
- 10 working days notice required (minimum)
- Police and Environmental Health can object

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.4: DESIGNATED PREMISES SUPERVISOR (DPS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
- Named individual on premises licence
- Must hold personal licence
- Day-to-day responsibility for alcohol sales
- Must authorise (or ensure authorisation of) every sale

Requirements:
- Must be named on premises licence
- Must hold valid personal licence
- Must give consent to being named
- Can only be one DPS per premises

Responsibilities:
- Ensuring compliance with licensing law
- Training staff
- Maintaining standards
- Point of contact for authorities

Authorising Sales:
- Every sale of alcohol must be authorised by:
  * The DPS, OR
  * Another personal licence holder, OR
  * Someone authorised by them
- Does not have to be on premises constantly
- But must have authorised sales to take place

Changes:
- DPS can be changed by premises licence holder
- Immediate effect in some cases
- Police can object to a new DPS

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.5: LICENSABLE ACTIVITIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Sale of Alcohol:
- Selling alcohol to the public
- Requires premises licence or TEN
- Sales must be authorised

Supply of Alcohol:
- Members' clubs supplying to members
- Club premises certificate

Regulated Entertainment:
- Live music (some exemptions)
- Recorded music (some exemptions)
- Performance of plays
- Films
- Indoor sporting events
- Boxing/wrestling
- Performance of dance

Late Night Refreshment:
- Supply of hot food or drink
- Between 11pm and 5am
- To the public
- For consumption on or off premises

Exemptions:
- Some activities exempt in certain circumstances
- Small live music venues
- Incidental music
- Check specific rules

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.6: CHALLENGE 21 AND CHALLENGE 25
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Challenge 21:
- Ask for ID if person looks UNDER 21
- Aim: prevent under-18 sales
- Gives 3-year margin for error

Challenge 25:
- Ask for ID if person looks UNDER 25
- Aim: prevent under-18 sales
- Gives 7-year margin for error
- More cautious approach
- Widely adopted as best practice

Why Challenge 25 is Better:
- Greater margin for error
- People often look older than they are
- Harder to guess ages accurately
- Provides better protection

Acceptable Forms of ID:
- Passport
- Photo driving licence (UK or EU)
- PASS accredited card (with PASS hologram)
- Military ID

Features to Check:
- Photograph matches person
- Date of birth confirms over 18
- Hologram present and genuine
- Card not expired
- Not damaged or altered

PASS Scheme:
- Proof of Age Standards Scheme
- Accredits proof of age cards
- Look for PASS hologram
- Indicates card meets standards

TOPIC 2.7.7: ALCOHOL-RELATED OFFENCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SECTION 141 - SALE TO A PERSON WHO IS DRUNK:
- Offence to sell or supply alcohol to drunk person
- Also offence to allow sale on relevant premises
- Up to £1,000 fine (level 3)
- Applies to any staff member who sells

SECTION 142 - OBTAINING ALCOHOL FOR A DRUNK PERSON:
- Offence to buy alcohol for someone who is drunk
- Applies to customers, not just staff

SECTION 143 - FAILURE TO LEAVE LICENSED PREMISES:
- Person asked to leave who refuses
- By licensee or their employee
- Offence to fail to leave
- Police can be called

SECTION 146 - SALE OF ALCOHOL TO CHILD:
- Offence to sell alcohol to under-18
- Up to £20,000 fine
- Personal licence can be revoked
- Premises licence can be reviewed

SECTION 147 - ALLOWING SALE TO CHILD:
- Offence for premises/personal licence holder
- To knowingly allow sale to under-18
- Same penalties

SECTION 147A - PERSISTENTLY SELLING TO CHILDREN:
- 2 or more sales to under-18s within 3 months
- Closure notice possible
- Serious consequences for venue

SECTION 149 - PURCHASE OF ALCOHOL BY/FOR CHILD:
- Offence for under-18 to buy alcohol
- Offence for adult to buy for under-18
- Exceptions: supervised table meal

SECTION 150 - CONSUMPTION BY CHILD:
- Offence to knowingly allow under-18 to consume alcohol
- On relevant premises
- Exceptions apply

SECTION 140 - ALLOWING DISORDERLY CONDUCT:
- Offence for responsible person
- To knowingly allow disorderly conduct
- On licensed premises

Signs of Drunkenness:
- Slurred speech
- Unsteady on feet
- Glazed eyes
- Aggressive or emotional behaviour
- Strong smell of alcohol
- Poor coordination
- Loss of inhibitions

Door Supervisor Role:
- Refuse entry to drunk persons
- Identify drunkenness on premises
- Report to bar staff (stop serving)
- Eject if necessary
- Document incidents

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.8: CHILDREN ON LICENSED PREMISES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

General Position:
- Under-18s can enter many licensed premises
- But restrictions apply to certain areas and times

Under-16s:
- Must be accompanied by adult in bar areas
- Cannot buy or consume alcohol
- May be restricted at certain times

16-17 Year Olds:
- Can be in bar areas unaccompanied
- Cannot buy alcohol
- CAN consume beer, wine, or cider with table meal
- If purchased by adult
- Accompanied by adult

Under-18s Generally:
- Cannot buy alcohol
- Cannot attempt to buy alcohol
- Cannot consume alcohol (except above exception)

Premises Restrictions:
- Some premises licensed for no under-18s
- Some areas restricted (nightclub floors)
- Some times restricted (after certain hour)
- Check premises licence conditions

Door Supervisor Role:
- Apply Challenge 25
- Refuse entry to under-18s where required
- Check ID rigorously
- Know venue's policy on children

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.9: MANDATORY CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Certain conditions apply to ALL premises licences:

Irresponsible Promotions BANNED:
- Games/activities encouraging excessive drinking
- Provision of unlimited alcohol for fixed price
- Prizes involving alcohol
- Drinking games
- "Dentist chairs" etc.
- Speed drinking

Exception: certain promotions allowed at specific times/for specific purposes

Free Tap Water:
- Must provide free drinking water
- On request
- To all customers

Age Verification Policy:
- Must have policy requiring production of ID
- From persons appearing under 18
- Before alcohol served

Small Measures:
- Must offer:
  * Half pint of beer/cider
  * 125ml glass of wine
  * 25ml or 35ml measure of spirits (whichever used)

Ban on Dispensing Alcohol Directly into Mouth:
- Cannot dispense alcohol directly into mouth of customer
- E.g., no "dentist chairs"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.10: POWERS OF ENTRY AND CLOSURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Police Powers of Entry:
- Police can enter licensed premises
- To check compliance
- To investigate offences
- No warrant needed during licensable activities

Licensing Authority Powers:
- Authorised officers can enter
- To check compliance
- Inspect records
- Test purchases

Closure Powers:
- Police can close premises immediately
- In cases of disorder or imminent disorder
- For public safety
- Up to 24 hours initially
- Can be extended

Review of Licence:
- Any interested party can request review
- Police, fire, environmental health, residents
- Licensing committee considers
- Can add conditions
- Can suspend
- Can revoke licence

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.7.11: DOOR SUPERVISOR ROLE IN LICENSING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Supporting the Four Objectives:

CRIME AND DISORDER:
- Searching customers
- Ejecting troublemakers
- Preventing violence
- Drug prevention
- Controlling access

PUBLIC SAFETY:
- Monitoring capacity
- Assisting evacuation
- First aid response
- Managing crowds

PUBLIC NUISANCE:
- Controlling noise at exit
- Dispersing customers quietly
- Preventing disturbance to neighbours

PROTECTING CHILDREN:
- Challenge 25 implementation
- Refusing under-age entry
- Spotting child exploitation
- Safeguarding vulnerable young people

Professional Standards:
- Maintain SIA licence
- Uphold licensing objectives
- Support venue compliance
- Document incidents properly
