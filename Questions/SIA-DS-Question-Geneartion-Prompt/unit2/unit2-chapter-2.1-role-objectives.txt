TOPIC 2.1.1: PRIMARY ROLE OF A DOOR SUPERVISOR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Core Purpose:
- Create and maintain a SAFE environment
- Safety is the PRIMARY objective - above profit or customer numbers
- Protect customers, staff, and the public
- Represent the venue professionally

First Point of Contact:
- Often the first person customers meet
- Set the tone for customer experience
- Balance security with customer service
- Professional and welcoming demeanour

Deterrent Effect:
- Visible security presence deters crime
- Professional appearance enhances deterrent
- Reputation of venue and door team matters
- Consistent, fair approach

Dual Role:
- Security professional AND customer service representative
- Not just "bouncers" - professional operatives
- Must balance firmness with friendliness
- Protect while being welcoming

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.2: CORE RESPONSIBILITIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Controlling Access:
- Managing entry and exit points
- Ensuring only authorised persons enter
- Checking tickets/guest lists where applicable
- Monitoring who enters and leaves

Verifying Age and Identity:
- Checking ID for age verification
- Challenge 25 policy (ask if looks under 25)
- Acceptable ID: passport, driving licence, PASS card
- Spotting fake or altered ID

Enforcing Venue Policies:
- Dress codes
- Behaviour standards
- No drugs policy
- Capacity limits
- Banned individuals

Monitoring Customer Behaviour:
- Observing for signs of trouble
- Identifying intoxication
- Spotting drug use/dealing
- Recognising vulnerability

Responding to Incidents:
- Dealing with altercations
- Managing ejections
- First aid situations
- Supporting colleagues

Assisting with Evacuations:
- Fire evacuation procedures
- Emergency response
- Directing customers to exits
- Preventing re-entry

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.3: ADMISSION POLICIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dress Codes:
- Venue sets dress code policy
- Must be applied consistently
- Cannot be discriminatory (Equality Act)
- Explain policy politely when refusing

Capacity Limits:
- Legal maximum occupancy
- Set by fire risk assessment
- NEVER exceed capacity
- One-in-one-out when full
- Criminal offence to exceed

Refusal of Entry:
- Right to refuse without giving reason
- But must not discriminate
- Common legitimate reasons:
  * Intoxication
  * Previous trouble/banned
  * Dress code
  * Capacity full
  * Underage
  * Aggressive behaviour
- Document refusals if problems anticipated

Banned Individuals:
- Maintain banned list
- Photos if available
- Share intelligence with other venues
- Reasons for banning documented

Discrimination Awareness:
- Cannot refuse based on protected characteristics
- Age, disability, race, religion, sex, sexual orientation, etc.
- Dress codes must not indirectly discriminate
- Consistent application is key

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.4: TEAMWORK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Communication with Colleagues:
- Clear and professional
- Radio communication protocols
- Hand signals where appropriate
- Brief and debrief

Briefings and Debriefings:
- Before shift: venue status, expected issues, VIPs
- After shift: incidents, concerns, handover
- Learning from each shift

Handover Procedures:
- Information about ongoing situations
- Persons of concern
- Incidents occurred
- Status of venue

Supporting Colleagues:
- Back up when needed
- Don't leave colleague alone in conflict
- Coordinate response
- Maintain coverage of venue

Division of Roles:
- Front door team
- Inside/floor team
- Roaming supervisors
- Know your position and stay there unless necessary

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.5: WORKING WITH EXTERNAL AGENCIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Police Liaison:
- Build positive relationships
- Share relevant intelligence
- Report serious incidents
- Cooperate with investigations

Emergency Services:
- Know when to call 999
- Provide clear information
- Clear access for responders
- Assist as directed

Local Authority:
- Licensing officers may visit
- Cooperate fully
- Know licence conditions

Door Supervisor Schemes:
- Pubwatch / Clubwatch
- Share intelligence between venues
- Banned lists shared
- Best practice sharing

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.6: VENUE KNOWLEDGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Essential Knowledge:
- Layout of entire premises
- All fire exits and escape routes
- Location of fire extinguishers
- First aid kit locations
- CCTV coverage
- Emergency procedures
- Capacity limits

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.7: PROFESSIONALISM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Appearance and Uniform:
- Clean, pressed uniform
- Good personal hygiene
- Professional grooming
- SIA licence displayed correctly

Conduct:
- Remain alert and vigilant
- No alcohol or drugs on duty
- Stay at designated position
- Professional language
- Punctuality and reliability

Confidentiality:
- Customer information
- Venue security measures
- Incident details
- Only share on need-to-know basis

What is the PRIMARY role of a door supervisor at a licensed premises?

the venue's business is part of the job, safety is always the overriding
