TOPIC 2.1.1: THE ROLE OF A SECURITY OFFICER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Functions:
- Protection of property and assets
- Protection of people (staff, visitors, public)
- Prevention and detection of crime
- Maintaining order and safety
- Deterrence through visible presence
- Reporting and recording incidents

Key Differences from Door Supervisors:
- Security officers typically work in NON-LICENSED premises
- Cannot work at venues where alcohol is the primary business
- Focus on static guarding, patrolling, access control
- Less emphasis on physical intervention
- Work environments: offices, retail, warehouses, construction sites

Types of Security Work:
- Static guarding (fixed position)
- Mobile patrols (covering multiple sites)
- Retail security (loss prevention)
- Corporate security (office buildings)
- Industrial security (factories, warehouses)
- Construction site security
- Event security (non-licensed events)
- Residential security (housing estates)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.2: PROFESSIONALISM AND CONDUCT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Professional Standards:
- Punctuality and reliability
- Appropriate dress and appearance
- Maintaining composure under pressure
- Representing employer and client professionally
- Honesty and integrity
- Confidentiality

Personal Presentation:
- Clean and well-maintained uniform
- Visible SIA licence badge
- Good personal hygiene
- Professional demeanour
- Appropriate language

Customer Service:
- Security officers are often first point of contact
- Polite and helpful attitude
- Clear communication
- Representing the client's business positively
- Balancing security with customer experience

Ethical Conduct:
- Not abusing position of authority
- Treating everyone fairly and without discrimination
- Reporting wrongdoing (including colleague misconduct)
- Not accepting bribes or inducements
- Maintaining boundaries

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.3: AUTHORITY AND LIMITATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Legal Powers:
- NO special legal powers beyond ordinary citizen
- Same powers as any member of the public
- Authority comes from property owner (delegated authority)
- Can request (not demand) cooperation
- Can deny entry to private premises
- Can ask people to leave private property

What Security Officers CAN Do:
- Ask for identification (request, not demand)
- Deny entry to private premises
- Request someone to leave
- Detain under citizen's arrest (strict conditions)
- Use reasonable force in limited circumstances
- Conduct searches WITH CONSENT
- Observe and report

What Security Officers CANNOT Do:
- Search without consent
- Detain without lawful reason
- Use excessive force
- Demand identification
- Make arrests for minor offences
- Act as police officers
- Impersonate police

Delegated Authority:
- Authority given by property owner/occupier
- Limited to specific premises
- Subject to conditions set by employer
- Does not extend beyond site boundaries
- Can be withdrawn at any time

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.4: DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Core Duties:
- Protecting premises from unauthorised access
- Preventing theft and damage
- Monitoring CCTV and alarm systems
- Conducting patrols
- Controlling access and egress
- Responding to incidents
- Reporting and documentation
- Liaising with emergency services

Duty of Care:
- Responsibility for safety of persons on site
- Includes staff, visitors, contractors, public
- Extends to trespassers in some circumstances
- Must not create additional hazards
- Take reasonable care to prevent foreseeable harm

Health and Safety Responsibilities:
- Follow site safety procedures
- Report hazards
- Use PPE when required
- Know emergency procedures
- Not take unnecessary risks
- Maintain safe working practices

Reporting Responsibilities:
- Report all incidents
- Complete daily occurrence logs
- Document suspicious activity
- Report security breaches
- Handover briefings between shifts
- Report defective equipment

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.5: WORKING RELATIONSHIPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Relationship with Client:
- Security officer represents the client
- Must understand client's requirements
- Follow client's policies and procedures
- Professional relationship
- Report issues through proper channels

Relationship with Employer:
- Follow company policies
- Report to designated supervisor
- Attend training as required
- Maintain licence and documentation
- Professional conduct at all times

Relationship with Police:
- Cooperate fully with police
- Provide information when requested
- Assist with investigations
- Hand over detained persons
- Preserve evidence
- Not obstruct police duties

Relationship with Public:
- Courteous and professional
- Firm but fair
- Explain decisions when appropriate
- Avoid confrontation where possible
- Represent security industry positively

Working with Colleagues:
- Teamwork and communication
- Reliable and trustworthy
- Share information appropriately
- Support each other
- Maintain professional standards

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.6: LONE WORKING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Definition:
- Working without direct supervision
- No colleagues in immediate vicinity
- Common in security work
- Increased personal risk

Risks of Lone Working:
- Personal safety concerns
- Delayed response to emergencies
- Vulnerability to attack
- Difficulty getting assistance
- Stress and isolation

Control Measures:
- Regular check-in procedures
- Communication devices (radio, phone)
- Panic alarms
- CCTV monitoring
- Clear procedures for emergencies
- Risk assessments
- Training

Personal Safety:
- Be aware of surroundings
- Know escape routes
- Don't take unnecessary risks
- Report concerns immediately
- Follow lone worker procedures
- Trust instincts

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.7: ASSIGNMENT INSTRUCTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose of Assignment Instructions:
- Define duties for specific site
- Provide site-specific information
- Set out procedures and protocols
- Emergency contact information
- Chain of command

Contents Typically Include:
- Site description and layout
- Post orders (specific duties)
- Patrol routes and frequency
- Access control procedures
- Emergency procedures
- Contact numbers
- Reporting requirements
- Client-specific policies

Importance of Following:
- Legal protection for officer
- Consistency of service
- Client expectations met
- Accountability
- Professional standards

Updating Assignment Instructions:
- Report changes or issues
- Suggest improvements
- Keep current and relevant
- Review regularly

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.8: CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Confidential Information:
- Client business information
- Security procedures and vulnerabilities
- Personal data of staff/visitors
- Incident details
- Alarm codes and access information

Duty of Confidentiality:
- Do not share sensitive information
- Only disclose to authorised persons
- Protect written and electronic records
- Secure disposal of documents
- Professional obligation

Data Protection Basics:
- Personal data must be protected
- Only collect what is necessary
- Do not share without authorisation
- Secure storage required
- Individual rights to access their data
- GDPR compliance

Consequences of Breach:
- Dismissal
- Legal action
- Damage to client
- Loss of SIA licence
- Criminal prosecution (serious cases)

What is one of the PRIMARY functions of a security officer?
