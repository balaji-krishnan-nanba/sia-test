WHY THIS CHAPTER MATTERS
------------------------
The SIA Close Protection licence permits holders to work as door supervisors
without a separate DS licence. CPOs may undertake door supervision work 
between CP contracts, work at events where their principal is attending, 
or supervise door teams at client premises. Understanding the door 
supervisor role is essential.

Question 1 [Easy]
Why can a Close Protection Operative work as a door supervisor without 
a separate licence?

A) Door supervision doesn't require a licence
B) The CP licence includes entitlement for door supervision work
C) CPOs are exempt from all licensing requirements
D) Door supervision is not a regulated activity

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: The SIA Close Protection licence includes entitlement to 
work as a door supervisor. This is because CP operatives are trained to 
a higher level that encompasses door supervision skills. CPOs do not need 
a separate door supervision licence to work legally in this role.
