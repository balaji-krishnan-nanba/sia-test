BACKGROUND CONTEXT
================================================================================

THE QUALIFICATION
-----------------
Level 2 Award for Working as a CCTV Operator (Public Space Surveillance)
- Regulates operators monitoring CCTV in public spaces
- Control room based work
- Town centre schemes, transport hubs, retail environments

THE EXAM
--------
Unit 2: Working as a CCTV Operator (Public Space Surveillance)
- 40 multiple choice questions in actual exam
- 60 minutes duration
- 70% pass mark (28 correct answers required)
- This chapter (2.5) is one of 6 chapters in Unit 2

WHY THIS CHAPTER MATTERS
------------------------
The core skill of a CCTV operator is identifying incidents and coordinating
appropriate response. Recognising suspicious behaviour, prioritising
effectively, providing accurate information to responders, and protecting
vulnerable people are essential competencies that directly impact public
safety.

TOPIC 2.5.1: IDENTIFYING INCIDENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Types of Incidents:

CRIMINAL ACTIVITY:
- Theft (shoplifting, robbery)
- Assault/violence
- Criminal damage
- Drug offences
- Weapons
- Vehicle crime
- Burglary

ANTI-SOCIAL BEHAVIOUR:
- Drunkenness
- Begging
- Street drinking
- Urinating in public
- Harassment
- Noise
- Threatening behaviour

MEDICAL EMERGENCIES:
- Collapse
- Heart attack signs
- Injury
- Unconsciousness
- Overdose

TRAFFIC INCIDENTS:
- Accidents
- Obstructions
- Dangerous driving
- Hit and run

FIRE/HAZARDS:
- Smoke
- Flames
- Gas leak signs
- Chemical spill
- Structural issues

MISSING/VULNERABLE PERSONS:
- Children alone
- Confused elderly
- Persons at risk
- Missing person alerts

SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY:
- Potential terrorism
- Hostile reconnaissance
- Unusual behaviour
- Surveillance of security

Recognising Incidents:
- Sudden crowd movement
- People running
- Groups gathering
- Someone on ground
- Emergency services arriving
- Smoke/flames
- Vehicle stopped unusually

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.5.2: BEHAVIOURAL INDICATORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Suspicious Behaviour:

GENERAL INDICATORS:
- Loitering without purpose
- Repeated visits
- Nervousness
- Looking for cameras
- Avoiding detection
- Out of place for location/time

THEFT INDICATORS:
- Watching staff/security
- Large bags/clothing
- Working in pairs
- Signal systems
- Distraction techniques
- Targeting high-value items

DRUG ACTIVITY:
- Brief contacts
- Small exchanges
- Multiple phones
- Money handling
- Meeting same people
- Leaving and returning

VIOLENCE PRECURSORS:
- Aggressive posturing
- Removing clothing
- Clenching fists
- Staring/pointing
- Following someone
- Gathering weapons (bottles)

RECONNAISSANCE:
- Photographing security
- Noting camera positions
- Testing security responses
- Asking unusual questions
- Making notes
- Timing activities

Interpreting Behaviour:
- Context matters
- May be innocent explanation
- Look for patterns
- Multiple indicators more significant
- Don't jump to conclusions
- Professional judgment

Cultural Awareness:
- Different norms
- Avoid stereotyping
- Behaviour vs appearance
- Focus on actions
- Fair and objective

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.5.3: PRIORITISATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Priority Levels:

IMMEDIATE/CRITICAL:
- Life at risk
- Serious injury
- Crime in progress (violent)
- Terrorism related
- Major incident

HIGH PRIORITY:
- Crime in progress (property)
- Vulnerable person at risk
- Serious disorder
- Emergency service request

MEDIUM PRIORITY:
- Anti-social behaviour
- Suspicious activity
- Minor disorder
- Partner requests

ROUTINE:
- General monitoring
- Minor incidents
- Information gathering
- Proactive patrol

Priority Assessment:

RISK TO LIFE:
- Always highest priority
- Immediate response
- All resources if needed
- Nothing more important

VULNERABILITY:
- Children
- Elderly
- Disabled
- Intoxicated
- Mental health
- Risk of exploitation

SERIOUSNESS OF OFFENCE:
- Violence > property
- Ongoing > concluded
- Multiple victims
- Weapons involved

TIME SENSITIVITY:
- In progress > historic
- Evidence being lost
- Offender leaving area
- Opportunity for arrest

Dealing with Multiple Incidents:
- Prioritise by risk
- Delegate if possible
- Alert partners
- Document all
- Review decisions

TOPIC 2.5.4: RESPONSE ACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediate Actions:

1. OBSERVE:
   - What is happening?
   - Who is involved?
   - Where exactly?
   - Weapons/injuries?
   - Direction of travel?

2. RECORD:
   - Ensure recording
   - Note camera numbers
   - Mark footage
   - Preserve evidence

3. ASSESS:
   - What response needed?
   - Who to alert?
   - Priority level?
   - Resources required?

4. COMMUNICATE:
   - Alert appropriate responder
   - Clear, accurate information
   - Location precise
   - Description detailed

5. TRACK:
   - Follow subject if mobile
   - Update location
   - Direction of travel
   - Hand over between cameras

6. DIRECT:
   - Guide responders
   - Shortest route
   - Current location
   - Updates as changed

7. DOCUMENT:
   - Complete incident log
   - All actions recorded
   - Times noted
   - Outcome documented

What NOT to Do:
- Panic
- Leave position (unless emergency)
- Ignore procedures
- Make assumptions
- Delay alerting
- Provide inaccurate information

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.5.5: DIRECTING RESPONDERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Information Responders Need:

LOCATION:
- Exact location
- Street name and number
- Landmarks
- Camera reference
- Access points

DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT:
- What is happening
- Type of incident
- Number involved
- Weapons
- Injuries

SUSPECT DESCRIPTION:
- Sex
- Ethnicity (IC codes)
- Age (approximate)
- Height
- Build
- Hair
- Clothing (top to bottom)
- Distinguishing features
- Direction of travel

VICTIM INFORMATION:
- Location
- Injuries
- Conscious/breathing
- Assistance needed

HAZARDS:
- Weapons present
- Multiple suspects
- Aggressive behaviour
- Environmental hazards

Tracking and Updates:
- Continuous monitoring
- Location updates
- "Now passing..."
- "Turned left onto..."
- "Stopped outside..."
- Changes in situation

Handing Over:
- When responder has visual
- Confirm correct person
- Continue until confirmed
- Return to monitoring

Good Practice:
- Calm, clear voice
- Structured information
- Answer questions
- Avoid jargon
- Confirm receipt

TOPIC 2.5.6: VULNERABLE PERSONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Who is Vulnerable:

CHILDREN:
- Unaccompanied
- Appearing lost
- Distressed
- With inappropriate adults
- At risk of exploitation

ELDERLY:
- Confused/disoriented
- Lost
- At risk of falls
- Targeted by criminals
- Medical emergencies

PEOPLE WITH DISABILITIES:
- Physical disabilities
- Learning disabilities
- Sensory impairments
- May need assistance

MENTAL HEALTH:
- Confused behaviour
- Self-harm risk
- Unpredictable
- May need specialist response

INTOXICATED:
- Alcohol
- Drugs
- Risk of harm
- Vulnerability to crime
- Medical risk

VICTIMS OF CRIME:
- Distressed
- Injured
- Needing support
- At continued risk

Responding to Vulnerable Persons:

CHILDREN:
- Alert police immediately
- Keep visual
- Don't lose them
- Look for adults
- Note any interactions

MISSING PERSONS:
- Check descriptions
- Alert if match
- Track and report
- Preserve footage
- Support search

SELF-HARM RISK:
- Alert emergency services
- Maintain observation
- Don't alarm them
- Support response
- Sensitive handling

MEDICAL EMERGENCY:
- Call ambulance
- Provide location
- Describe condition
- Guide responders
- Preserve dignity

Dignity and Respect:
- Everyone deserves respect
- Don't judge
- Sensitive monitoring
- Appropriate response
- Confidentiality

TOPIC 2.5.7: TERRORISM AWARENESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Recognising Terrorist Threats:

HOSTILE RECONNAISSANCE:
- Photographing security
- Noting CCTV positions
- Timing patrols
- Testing responses
- Mapping locations
- Observing crowds

SUSPICIOUS PACKAGES:
- Unattended bags
- Unusual items
- Wrong place
- No owner nearby
- Signs of concealment

SUSPICIOUS VEHICLES:
- Parked unusually
- Unattended
- Wrong place
- Blocked windows
- Heavy rear
- Monitoring location

SUSPICIOUS BEHAVIOUR:
- Nervousness
- Inappropriate clothing
- Avoiding security
- Leaving quickly
- Multiple visits

Attack Methods Awareness:
- Vehicle attacks
- Bladed weapons
- Firearms
- IEDs (bombs)
- CBRN (chemical, biological)
- Multiple attackers

The HOT Protocol:
- H - Has it been HIDDEN?
- O - Is it OBVIOUSLY suspicious?
- T - Is it TYPICAL for this location?

If answers suggest concern = REPORT

Run, Hide, Tell:

RUN:
- If safe to do so
- Leave belongings
- Insist others leave
- Don't use lifts

HIDE:
- If cannot run
- Find cover
- Lock doors
- Silence phone
- Don't draw attention

TELL:
- Call 999 when safe
- What you saw
- Location
- Description of attackers
- Direction of travel

CCTV Operator Role in Terrorism:
- Alert police immediately
- Provide real-time intelligence
- Track attackers if safe
- Direct responders
- Preserve ALL footage
- Stay calm, professional
- Support coordination

Reporting:
- Anti-Terrorist Hotline: 0800 789 321
- In emergency: 999
- Online: ACT website
- Don't ignore suspicions
- Better to report than not

TOPIC 2.5.8: POST-INCIDENT ACTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediate Post-Incident:

PRESERVE EVIDENCE:
- Mark footage immediately
- Note camera numbers
- Note time codes
- Protect from overwriting
- Multiple cameras

COMPLETE DOCUMENTATION:
- Full incident log
- Detailed description
- All actions taken
- Times and outcomes
- References

COMMUNICATIONS:
- Update partners
- Confirm status
- Outstanding actions
- Handover if shift ending

Review:
- What happened?
- Response effective?
- Lessons learned?
- Procedure updates needed?

Evidence Requests:
- Expect police requests
- Know procedure
- Preserve footage
- Maintain chain of custody
- Prepare for statement

Staff Welfare:
- Distressing incidents affect staff
- Supervisor support
- Debriefing if needed
- Counselling available
- Don't struggle alone

Recognising Stress:
- Replaying incident mentally
- Difficulty sleeping
- Anxiety
- Mood changes
- Physical symptoms

Seeking Support:
- Talk to supervisor
- Occupational health
- Counselling services
- Employee assistance
- Colleagues

What should ALWAYS be the highest priority for a CCTV operator?
