TOPIC 2.1.1: PURPOSE OF PUBLIC SPACE CCTV
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Purposes:

CRIME PREVENTION:
- Deterrent effect
- Visible cameras discourage offending
- Potential offenders know they're watched
- Reduces opportunistic crime

CRIME DETECTION:
- Identifying offenders
- Gathering evidence
- Real-time observation
- Post-incident investigation

PUBLIC REASSURANCE:
- People feel safer
- Confidence in public spaces
- Visible commitment to safety
- Community trust

COMMUNITY SAFETY:
- Supporting vulnerable people
- Missing persons
- Medical emergencies
- Anti-social behaviour reduction

EVIDENCE GATHERING:
- Criminal prosecutions
- Civil proceedings
- Insurance claims
- Coroner's inquests

SUPPORTING EMERGENCY SERVICES:
- Directing police to incidents
- Guiding ambulance crews
- Monitoring fire evacuations
- Coordinating responses

Benefits of Public Space CCTV:
- Reduces fear of crime
- Assists police investigations
- Helps locate missing/vulnerable persons
- Monitors crowds and events
- Supports licensing enforcement
- Traffic management
- Economic benefits (safer town centres)

Limitations of CCTV:
- Cannot prevent all crime
- Displacement (crime moves elsewhere)
- Privacy concerns
- Requires monitoring to be effective
- Weather/lighting affects quality
- Cannot cover everywhere
- Maintenance costs

TOPIC 2.1.6: PROACTIVE VS REACTIVE MONITORING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

REACTIVE MONITORING:
- Responding to incidents reported
- Alarm activations
- Police/partner requests
- Following up alerts
- Waiting for things to happen

PROACTIVE MONITORING:
- Actively searching for incidents
- Scanning for suspicious behaviour
- Anticipating problems
- Preventive observation
- Intelligence-led monitoring

Examples of Proactive Monitoring:
- Watching known hotspots
- Monitoring during peak times
- Observing known offenders
- Checking vulnerable locations
- Seasonal awareness (Christmas shopping)

Why Proactive is Better:
- Prevents crimes before they happen
- Earlier intervention
- Better outcomes
- Demonstrates value
- Builds partner confidence

Balancing Both Approaches:
- Resources limited
- Must respond to requests
- But also patrol proactively
- Priority systems help
- Training and experience

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.7: SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS OF THE ROLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What CCTV Operators CAN Do:
- Monitor public spaces
- Operate camera equipment
- Record footage
- Communicate with partners
- Alert emergency services
- Preserve evidence
- Provide descriptions
- Direct responders
- Document incidents

What CCTV Operators CANNOT Do:
- Make arrests
- Physically intervene
- Enter premises
- Search people
- Demand identification
- Use force
- Act as police officers
- Access private property

Professional Boundaries:
- Stay within your role
- Don't exceed authority
- Support, don't replace police
- Observe and report
- Let responders handle

When to Escalate:
- Serious crime in progress
- Life at risk
- Beyond your capability
- Need specialist response
- Uncertain how to proceed

Limitations of Observation:
- Can only see what cameras cover
- Blind spots exist
- Weather affects visibility
- Night vision limitations
- Distance from cameras
- Cannot hear conversations

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.1.8: PROFESSIONALISM AND ETHICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Professional Conduct:
- Follow all procedures
- Maintain confidentiality
- Treat everyone fairly
- No personal use of systems
- Appropriate behaviour

Ethical Considerations:

PRIVACY:
- Respect individuals' privacy
- Don't observe unnecessarily
- Proportionate monitoring
- No voyeurism
- Professional detachment

FAIRNESS:
- No discrimination
- Equal treatment
- No targeting groups
- Objective monitoring

HONESTY:
- Accurate reporting
- Don't falsify records
- Admit mistakes
- Truthful statements

CONFIDENTIALITY:
- Don't share footage inappropriately
- No social media
- Protect personal data
- Need-to-know basis

Misuse of Systems - Examples:
- Watching friends/family
- Tracking ex-partners
- Sharing footage online
- Using for personal reasons
- Inappropriate viewing

Consequences of Misconduct:
- Disciplinary action
- Dismissal
- Loss of SIA licence
- Criminal prosecution
- Civil liability
