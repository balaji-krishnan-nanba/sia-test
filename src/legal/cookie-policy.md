## 1. What Are Cookies?

### 1.1 Definition

**Cookies** are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide information to website owners, and improve your browsing experience.

### 1.2 How Cookies Work

When you visit our Platform:

1. Your browser requests a webpage from our server
2. Our server sends the webpage along with a cookie
3. Your browser stores the cookie on your device
4. When you visit again, your browser sends the cookie back to our server
5. This allows us to recognize you and remember your preferences

### 1.3 Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

- Keep you logged in to your account
- Remember your preferences and settings
- Understand how you use our Platform
- Improve your user experience
- Analyze Platform performance and usage patterns
- Ensure security and prevent fraud

## 2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use different types of cookies for various purposes:

### 2.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

**Purpose:** These cookies are absolutely necessary for the Platform to function properly.

**What they do:**
- Authenticate your login session
- Remember you're logged in as you navigate between pages
- Maintain security and prevent unauthorized access
- Enable core functionality like accessing your account and progress data

**Duration:** Session cookies (deleted when you close your browser)

**Examples:**
- `auth_token` - Keeps you logged in
- `session_id` - Maintains your session
- `csrf_token` - Protects against cross-site request forgery

**Can you disable them?** No - the Platform will not function properly without these cookies.

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interest (necessary for service delivery)

---

### 2.2 Analytics Cookies (Performance)

**Purpose:** These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Platform.

**What they do:**
- Track which pages you visit
- Measure how long you spend on each page
- Identify which features you use most
- Detect technical errors and performance issues
- Help us improve the Platform based on usage patterns

**Duration:** Up to 2 years

**Examples:**
- Google Analytics cookies:
  - `_ga` - Distinguishes unique users (2 years)
  - `_gid` - Distinguishes unique users (24 hours)
  - `_gat` - Throttles request rate (1 minute)

**Data collected:**
- Pages visited
- Time spent on Platform
- Device type and browser
- General location (city/country level, not precise)
- Referral source (how you found us)

**Can you disable them?** Yes - see Section 5 for instructions.

**Legal basis:** Consent (you can opt out)

---

### 2.3 Functional Cookies (Preference)

**Purpose:** These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization.

**What they do:**
- Remember your preferences (e.g., study mode, question display settings)
- Save your quiz settings and filters
- Remember your dashboard layout preferences
- Store your language preference (if applicable)

**Duration:** Up to 1 year

**Examples:**
- `user_preferences` - Stores your settings
- `quiz_mode` - Remembers your preferred quiz mode
- `theme_preference` - Dark/light mode preference (if implemented)

**Can you disable them?** Yes, but the Platform may not remember your preferences.

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interest (improving user experience)

---

### 2.4 Authentication Cookies (OAuth)

**Purpose:** If you sign in with Google OAuth, Google sets cookies for authentication.

**What they do:**
- Authenticate your Google account
- Enable single sign-on functionality
- Maintain your Google login session

**Duration:** Varies (set by Google)

**Third-party:** These cookies are set by Google and subject to Google's privacy policy.

**Can you disable them?** You can manage Google cookies in your Google account settings.

**Legal basis:** Necessary for providing OAuth authentication service

## 5. How to Manage and Disable Cookies

### 5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our Platform:

- You'll see a cookie consent banner
- You can choose to accept or decline non-essential cookies
- Essential cookies will be used regardless (necessary for functionality)

To change your preferences later:
- Visit our Cookie Settings page (link in footer)
- Or clear your browser cookies (see Section 5.2)

---

### 5.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

#### Google Chrome
1. Settings → Privacy and Security → Cookies and other site data
2. Choose "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"
3. Click "See all cookies and site data" to manage individual cookies

#### Mozilla Firefox
1. Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
2. Choose "Delete cookies and site data when Firefox is closed"
3. Or use "Manage Data" to remove specific cookies

#### Safari (macOS)
1. Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
2. Choose "Block all cookies" or manage individually
3. Click "Manage Website Data" to remove specific cookies

#### Microsoft Edge
1. Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies
2. Choose "Block third-party cookies" or "Block all cookies"
3. Click "Manage and delete cookies and site data"

---

### 5.3 Third-Party Opt-Outs

**Google Analytics:**
- Install the [Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)

**Google Ads (if applicable in future):**
- Visit [Google Ads Settings](https://adssettings.google.com/)

---

### 5.4 Effect of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies:

**Essential Cookies:**
- ❌ You cannot use the Platform (login will not work)
- ❌ Your session will not be maintained
- ❌ Security features will be disabled

**Analytics Cookies:**
- ✅ Platform will function normally
- ℹ️ We won't collect usage data from your visits
- ℹ️ Helps us improve, but not required

**Functional Cookies:**
- ✅ Platform will function normally
- ⚠️ Your preferences won't be saved
- ⚠️ You'll need to reset settings each visit

## 6. Cookie Duration and Expiry

### 6.1 Session Cookies

**Lifetime:** Deleted when you close your browser

**Used for:**
- Authentication
- Temporary session data
- Security tokens

### 6.2 Persistent Cookies

**Lifetime:** Remain on your device for a set period (or until you delete them)

**Duration:**
- Authentication: 30 days (or until you log out)
- Analytics: Up to 2 years
- Preferences: Up to 1 year

**Used for:**
- "Remember me" functionality
- Saved preferences
- Analytics and performance tracking

---

You can delete cookies at any time:

1. **Through browser settings** (see Section 5.2)
2. **Using browser extensions** (e.g., Cookie AutoDelete)
3. **Through our Platform** - Log out to clear session cookies

**Note:** Deleting cookies will log you out and reset your preferences.
