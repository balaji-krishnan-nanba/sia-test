TOPIC 4.4.1: PRINCIPLES OF PROTECTIVE SECURITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is Protective Security:
- Measures to protect against threats
- Physical and procedural
- Personnel security
- Information security
- Integrated approach

The 4 D's of Protective Security:

DETER:
- Discourage attack
- Visible security
- Professional presence
- Message: "Not easy target"

DETECT:
- Identify threats early
- Surveillance awareness
- Suspicious activity
- Early warning systems

DELAY:
- Slow down attack
- Physical barriers
- Access control
- Time to respond

DENY:
- Prevent success
- Stop the attack
- Protect target
- Respond effectively

Additional Consideration:

RESPOND:
- React to incident
- Emergency procedures
- Recovery
- Continuity

Layered Security (Defence in Depth):
- Multiple layers of protection
- Each layer adds security
- Failure of one doesn't compromise all
- Complementary measures
- Inner layers strongest

Protective Security Planning:

ASSESS:
- What are the threats?
- What are vulnerabilities?
- What is at risk?

IMPLEMENT:
- Appropriate measures
- Proportionate to threat
- Practical and sustainable

REVIEW:
- Regular assessment
- Adapt to changes
- Continuous improvement

What do the 4 D's of protective security stand for?
