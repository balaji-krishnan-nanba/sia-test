TOPIC 1.1.1: HISTORY AND EVOLUTION OF CLOSE PROTECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Origins:
- Ancient times: Praetorian Guard (Roman emperors)
- Royal bodyguards throughout history
- Modern CP emerged post-WWII
- Growth in 1970s-80s due to terrorism
- UK private sector growth from 1990s onwards

Evolution in the UK:
- Originally unregulated
- High-profile incidents drove change
- Private Security Industry Act 2001
- SIA regulation from 2003
- CP licensing introduced 2006
- Continuous professional development
- April 2022 - major syllabus changes

"The Circuit":
- Industry term for CP work
- Network of operatives and employers
- International scope
- Reputation-based employment
- Word of mouth important
- Professional community

Modern Close Protection:
- Highly professionalised
- Regulated industry
- Technology integrated
- Intelligence-led
- Risk-based approach
- International standards

TOPIC 1.1.3: THE SECURITY INDUSTRY AUTHORITY (SIA) AND REGULATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The SIA:
- Regulatory body for private security
- Established under PSIA 2001
- Reports to Home Office
- Issues licences
- Sets training standards
- Enforces compliance

Private Security Industry Act 2001:
- Created the SIA
- Defined licensable activities
- Close protection is licensable
- Criminal offences for non-compliance
- Working without licence: up to 5 years imprisonment
- Employing unlicensed: up to 5 years imprisonment

Close Protection Licence:
- Front-line licence
- Allows guarding of individuals
- Also covers door supervision work
- Also covers security guard work
- Valid for 3 years
- Cost: £184

Who Needs a CP Licence:
- Anyone providing close protection under contract
- Protecting individuals from assault/injury
- Part of licensable activity
- "Contract for services" requirement

Exemptions:
- In-house employees (limited)
- Government agencies (police, military)
- Specific exemptions under PSIA 2001
- Crown servants in course of duties

Licence Integration:
- CP licence covers DS and SG work
- Most comprehensive licence
- Can work multiple roles
- Better employment flexibility
