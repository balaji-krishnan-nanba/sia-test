BACKGROUND CONTEXT

WHAT IS UNIT 3?
---------------
Unit 3 focuses on Conflict Management - the skills needed to avoid, defuse, 
and resolve conflict situations without resorting to physical intervention.

THE EXAM
--------
Unit 3: Conflict Management
- 20 multiple choice questions in actual exam
- 30 minutes duration
- 70% pass mark (14 correct answers required)
- This chapter (3.4) is one of 4 chapters in Unit 3

KEY PRINCIPLE
-------------
What happens after conflict is as important as managing the conflict itself.
Proper post-incident procedures protect you legally, help you learn and 
improve, and ensure you're looking after your own wellbeing.

TOPIC 3.4.1: IMMEDIATE AFTERMATH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Secure the Scene:
- Ensure safety of all persons
- Separate parties if still present
- Prevent escalation or resumption
- Check for injuries

Medical Attention:
- Assess if anyone injured
- First aid if trained and needed
- Call ambulance if serious
- Record injuries observed

Evidence Preservation:
- Secure any weapons/evidence
- Don't touch or move items if possible
- Note positions of items/people
- Request CCTV preservation

Witnesses:
- Identify witnesses
- Get contact details
- Ask them to stay if possible
- Keep witnesses separate

Return to Normal:
- Resume normal operations
- Reassure other customers
- Monitor for further trouble
- Maintain professional presence

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.2: DOCUMENTATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Why Document:
- Legal protection
- Evidence for prosecution
- Defence against claims
- Learning and improvement
- Regulatory compliance

What to Document:

DATE AND TIME:
- When incident occurred
- When you became aware
- When actions taken
- When incident ended

LOCATION:
- Specific location
- Area of venue
- Relevant features

PEOPLE INVOLVED:
- Descriptions (detailed)
- Names if known
- Role in incident
- Contact details

WHAT HAPPENED:
- Sequence of events
- What was said
- What was done
- Chronological order

YOUR ACTIONS:
- What you did
- When you did it
- Why you did it
- Force used (if any)

WITNESSES:
- Names and contacts
- What they saw
- Where they were

INJURIES:
- Who was injured
- Nature of injuries
- Treatment given
- Medical assistance

EVIDENCE:
- Items found/seized
- CCTV relevant times
- Property damage

Documentation Principles:
- FACTUAL: What you saw/heard, not opinion
- ACCURATE: Correct details
- TIMELY: As soon as possible
- COMPLETE: All relevant information
- LEGIBLE: Clear writing
- PROFESSIONAL: Appropriate language

ELBOWS Method:
- Event: What happened
- Location: Where
- Brought to attention by: How you became aware
- Observations: What YOU saw/heard
- Witnesses: Who else saw
- Subjects: People involved (descriptions)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.3: REPORTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Who to Report To:

MANAGEMENT:
- All incidents
- Immediately for serious
- End of shift for minor
- Follow venue procedure

POLICE:
- Criminal offences
- Serious violence
- Weapons
- Drugs (dealing)
- Any arrestable offence

LICENSING AUTHORITY:
- Serious incidents may require
- As per licence conditions
- Usually via management

SIA:
- If licence-related issue
- If arrest/conviction
- As required by regulations

RIDDOR (if applicable):
- Serious injury to employee
- Violence resulting in over-7-day absence
- Via employer

Report Content:
- Factual summary
- Key details
- Your involvement
- Outcome
- Supporting evidence reference

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.4: DEBRIEFING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is Debriefing:
- Review of incident after it happens
- What went well
- What could improve
- Learning opportunity

Types of Debrief:

HOT DEBRIEF:
- Immediately after incident
- Quick review
- Key points only
- While memory fresh
- Before end of shift

COLD DEBRIEF:
- Later, more thorough
- After emotions settled
- With management/team
- Detailed analysis
- Training opportunity

Debrief Content:

WHAT HAPPENED:
- Sequence of events
- Factual account
- Timeline

WHAT WENT WELL:
- Effective actions
- Good decisions
- Teamwork

WHAT COULD IMPROVE:
- Mistakes made
- Better approaches
- Missed opportunities

LESSONS LEARNED:
- What to do differently
- Training needs
- Procedure changes

ACTION POINTS:
- Specific improvements
- Who responsible
- Timeframe

Benefits of Debriefing:
- Continuous improvement
- Team learning
- Identifying training needs
- Process improvements
- Psychological processing
- Preparation for similar incidents

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.5: EMOTIONAL IMPACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Conflict Affects You:
- Even if you appear fine
- Adrenaline and stress hormones
- Can affect you later
- Normal human response

Immediate Effects:
- Adrenaline surge
- Increased heart rate
- Shakiness
- Difficulty concentrating
- Replaying incident
- Heightened alertness

Short-Term Effects:
- Difficulty sleeping
- Irritability
- Thinking about incident
- Mood changes
- Physical tension

Longer-Term Effects (if not addressed):
- Anxiety
- Depression
- Burnout
- Post-traumatic stress
- Physical health impact

Why This Matters:
- Affects job performance
- Affects personal life
- Affects relationships
- Affects health
- Can compound over time

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.6: SELF-CARE AND WELLBEING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Recognise Your Feelings:
- Accept emotional response is normal
- Don't suppress or deny
- Talk about it
- Seek support if needed

Immediate Self-Care:

PHYSICAL:
- Deep breathing
- Movement/exercise
- Proper rest
- Hydration and nutrition
- Avoid excess alcohol

PSYCHOLOGICAL:
- Talk to colleagues
- Debrief formally
- Acknowledge the difficulty
- Don't ruminate alone

PRACTICAL:
- Complete documentation
- Hand over properly
- Don't leave with unfinished business

Ongoing Self-Care:

MAINTAIN BOUNDARIES:
- Work-life separation
- Don't take work home mentally
- Time for rest and recovery
- Personal life matters

BUILD RESILIENCE:
- Regular exercise
- Sleep hygiene
- Healthy eating
- Social connections
- Hobbies and interests

KNOW YOUR LIMITS:
- Recognise when struggling
- Don't push through indefinitely
- Seek help early
- No shame in support

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.7: SUPPORT SYSTEMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Colleague Support:
- Talk to trusted colleagues
- Shared experience
- Practical advice
- Emotional support
- Mutual debriefing

Management Support:
- Report concerns
- Request support
- Time off if needed
- Changes to duties temporarily

Professional Support:

COUNSELLING:
- Available through some employers
- Employee Assistance Programmes (EAP)
- Private counselling
- NHS mental health services

OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH:
- Workplace health support
- Fitness for work
- Adjustments if needed

When to Seek Help:
- Symptoms persisting
- Affecting daily life
- Affecting work performance
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Relationship problems
- Substance misuse developing

Don't Ignore Warning Signs:
- Persistent sleep problems
- Constant anxiety
- Flashbacks
- Avoidance of work
- Emotional numbness
- Irritability/anger issues

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.8: LEARNING FROM INCIDENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Every Incident is Learning Opportunity:
- What worked
- What didn't work
- What could be done differently
- Systemic improvements

Individual Learning:
- Reflect on your actions
- Honest self-assessment
- Skills to develop
- Knowledge gaps

Team Learning:
- Share experiences
- Learn from colleagues
- Collective improvement
- Better coordination

Organisational Learning:
- Policy improvements
- Procedure updates
- Training enhancements
- Environmental changes

Feedback Loop:
- Incident → Review → Learning → Implementation → Better Practice
- Continuous cycle
- Culture of improvement

Avoid Blame Culture:
- Focus on learning, not blame
- Honest reporting
- Safe to admit mistakes
- Constructive improvement

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 3.4.9: LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS POST-INCIDENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Potential Legal Outcomes:

CRIMINAL PROCEEDINGS:
- Police investigation
- Statements required
- May be witness
- May be accused
- Court appearance possible

CIVIL CLAIMS:
- Compensation claims against you/venue
- False imprisonment
- Assault claims
- Negligence

REGULATORY ACTION:
- SIA investigation
- Licence review
- Conditions or revocation

EMPLOYMENT CONSEQUENCES:
- Disciplinary action
- Dismissal
- Reference impact

Protecting Yourself:

ACCURATE DOCUMENTATION:
- Your best defence
- Record what happened
- Record why you acted
- Record what you saw

WITNESS STATEMENTS:
- Colleagues who saw
- Independent witnesses
- Get them early (memory fades)

CCTV:
- Ensure preservation
- Know what's recorded
- Request copy if needed

LEGAL ADVICE:
- If accused of offence
- If facing claim
- Before making detailed statement to police
- Know your rights

Professional Conduct:
- Don't discuss case on social media
- Don't discuss with other witnesses
- Follow legal advice
- Maintain professionalism

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Learning from Experience:
- Each incident adds to experience
- Reflect and learn
- Don't repeat mistakes
- Build expertise

Training Opportunities:
- Additional courses
- Refresher training
- Specialist skills
- First aid, conflict management

Staying Current:
- Law changes
- Best practice updates
- New techniques
- Industry developments

Professional Standards:
- Uphold industry reputation
- Continuous improvement
- Exceed minimum standards
- Role model for others

Question 1 [Easy]
What is a "hot debrief"?

A) A debrief conducted in a heated argument
B) A debrief conducted immediately after an incident
C) A debrief with management present
D) A debrief that results in disciplinary action

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: A hot debrief is conducted immediately after an incident while 
memory is fresh and emotions are still present. It provides a quick review 
of key points. A cold debrief occurs later and is more thorough, after 
emotions have settled.
