BACKGROUND CONTEXT
================================================================================

WHAT IS THE SIA?
----------------
The Security Industry Authority (SIA) is the UK government body responsible for 
regulating the private security industry under the Private Security Industry 
Act 2001. The SIA:

- Issues licences to individuals working in regulated security roles
- Sets standards for training and qualifications
- Maintains a public register of approved contractors (ACS)
- Has powers to prosecute unlicensed operatives and their employers
- Reports to the Home Office

The SIA does NOT provide training or write exam papers.

WHAT IS A DOOR SUPERVISOR?
--------------------------
A Door Supervisor is a licensed security operative who works at licensed 
premises (pubs, clubs, bars, entertainment venues). They must hold a valid 
SIA licence to work legally.

TOPIC 1.1.1: THE SECURITY INDUSTRY AUTHORITY (SIA)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Facts:
- Established under Private Security Industry Act 2001
- Government body reporting to the Home Office
- Purpose: regulate private security industry, reduce criminality
- Came into effect in 2003 (licensing began)

SIA Powers:
- Issue licences to individuals
- Set standards for training
- Prosecute offenders (unlicensed workers, employers)
- Inspect security companies
- Revoke licences
- Maintain public register of licence holders

What SIA Does NOT Do:
- Does NOT provide training courses
- Does NOT write exam papers
- Does NOT employ security operatives
- Does NOT run security companies

SIA's Aims:
- Protect the public
- Raise standards in the industry
- Reduce criminal activity
- Ensure only fit and proper persons work in security

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.2: THE PRIVATE SECURITY INDUSTRY ACT 2001
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Provisions:
- Created the SIA
- Established the licensing regime
- Defined "regulated activities" requiring a licence
- Created criminal offences for non-compliance

Regulated Activities (Require SIA Licence):
- Manned guarding (security guards)
- Door supervision
- Close protection (bodyguarding)
- Cash and valuables in transit
- Public space surveillance (CCTV)
- Key holding

Exemptions:
- In-house security (employees protecting own employer's premises)
  * BUT only if not contracted out to other companies
- Certain government roles
- Volunteer stewards (limited activities)

Penalties for Offences:
- Working without a licence: up to 5 years imprisonment AND/OR unlimited fine
- Deploying unlicensed staff: up to 5 years imprisonment AND/OR unlimited fine
- Using false documents: criminal offence
- Impersonating SIA official: criminal offence

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.3: LICENCE TYPES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Door Supervisor (DS):
- Required for controlling entry to licensed premises
- Front-line licence (must be displayed)

Security Guard (SG):
- Guarding premises, property, persons
- Includes retail security, corporate security

CCTV Operator (Public Space Surveillance - PSS):
- Monitoring CCTV in public spaces
- Does NOT apply to private/in-house CCTV

Close Protection (CP):
- Bodyguarding individuals
- High-risk personal protection

Cash and Valuables in Transit (CVIT):
- Transporting cash, valuables, precious items
- Armoured vehicle crews

Key Holding:
- Responding to alarm activations
- Holding keys for client premises
- Often combined with other roles

Vehicle Immobiliser (VI):
- Historical licence type
- Clamping on private land
- Now largely obsolete due to law changes

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.4: THE SIA LICENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Licence Validity:
- Valid for 3 years from date of issue
- Must be renewed before expiry
- Apply for renewal before licence expires to avoid gap

Licence Display:
- Must be displayed when working
- Front-facing (visible to public)
- In approved armband or holder
- Cannot be hidden or obscured

Licence Contents:
- Photograph of holder
- Name of holder
- Licence number (unique identifier)
- Expiry date
- Licence type/role
- SIA hologram (security feature)

Licence Number Format:
- Unique to each licence holder
- Can be verified on SIA public register
- Employers can check validity online

Reporting Changes to SIA:
- Change of name: must report
- Change of address: must report
- Criminal convictions: must report
- Failure to report: can lead to revocation

Lost or Stolen Licence:
- Report to SIA immediately
- Apply for replacement
- Temporary licence may be issued
- Do not work without valid licence

Licence Conditions:
- Must not work outside licensed role
- Must display when working
- Must report changes
- Must behave professionally

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.5: CRIMINAL RECORD CHECKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service):
- Criminal record check required for all licence applicants
- Enhanced DBS check for front-line licences
- Checks spent and unspent convictions
- Also checks police intelligence (soft information)

What Disqualifies Applicants:
- Serious violent offences
- Sexual offences
- Drug trafficking
- Fraud and dishonesty
- Recent relevant offences

Assessment:
- SIA considers each case individually
- Considers: nature of offence, time elapsed, rehabilitation
- Not all convictions automatically disqualify
- Right to appeal decisions

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.6: APPROVED CONTRACTOR SCHEME (ACS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What is ACS:
- Voluntary scheme for security companies
- Demonstrates quality and professionalism
- SIA assesses and approves companies
- Regular audits and assessments

Benefits for Companies:
- Demonstrates quality to clients
- Preferred for government contracts
- Reduced licence fees for employees
- Marketing advantage
- Shows commitment to standards

ACS Standards:
- Management of operations
- Staff training and development
- Fair treatment of employees
- Quality of service delivery
- Health and safety compliance

Public Register:
- All ACS companies listed publicly
- Clients can verify ACS status
- Demonstrates credibility

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.7: OFFENCES AND PENALTIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Working Without a Licence (Section 3):
- Criminal offence
- Up to 5 years imprisonment
- Unlimited fine
- Applies to the individual worker

Deploying Unlicensed Staff (Section 5):
- Criminal offence
- Up to 5 years imprisonment
- Unlimited fine
- Applies to employer/manager

Using False Documents:
- Criminal offence
- Forged licence, fake ID
- Serious penalties

Section 16 Offence:
- Falsely claiming SIA endorsement or approval
- Claiming to be "SIA approved" when not
- Up to £5,000 fine
- Applies to businesses making false claims

Licence Revocation:
- SIA can revoke licence at any time
- Reasons: criminal conviction, misconduct, false information
- Right to appeal

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 1.1.8: PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Expected Conduct:
- Act with integrity and honesty
- Treat public with respect
- Maintain professional appearance
- Be punctual and reliable
- Follow employer policies
- Uphold the law

Responsibilities:
- Only work within licensed role
- Display licence when working
- Report incidents appropriately
- Maintain skills and knowledge
- Represent industry positively

Continuous Professional Development:
- Keep knowledge up to date
- Attend refresher training
- Learn from incidents
- Stay informed of legal changes
