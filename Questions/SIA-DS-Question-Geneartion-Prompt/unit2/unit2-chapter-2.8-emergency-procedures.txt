WHY THIS CHAPTER MATTERS
------------------------
Door supervisors are often first responders in emergencies at venues.
They must know how to respond to fire, medical emergencies, violence,
and terrorism. Their actions can save lives and minimise harm.

NOTE: Unit 1 covers fire safety and terrorism in detail. This chapter 
focuses on the door supervisor's specific role in venue emergencies.

TOPIC 2.8.1: TYPES OF EMERGENCIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Common Emergencies at Venues:
- Fire
- Medical emergencies (collapse, injury, overdose)
- Serious violence (glassing, stabbing)
- Terrorist incident
- Bomb threat
- Gas leak
- Power failure
- Structural damage
- Severe weather
- Crowd surge/crush

Door Supervisor Preparedness:
- Know venue emergency procedures BEFORE shift
- Know location of all exits
- Know fire equipment locations
- Know first aid kit locations
- Know assembly point
- Know who makes key decisions

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.2: FIRE EVACUATION - DOOR SUPERVISOR ROLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Before an Emergency:
- Familiarise with venue layout
- Know ALL fire exits
- Know evacuation routes
- Know assembly point location
- Check fire exits are clear and unlocked

On Hearing Fire Alarm:

1. BEGIN EVACUATION:
- Clear your area immediately
- Direct customers to nearest exit
- Use clear, calm instructions

2. COMMUNICATION:
- "Please leave by the nearest exit"
- "Do not use the lift"
- "Leave your belongings"
- Stay calm - customers take cues from you

3. ASSIST THOSE NEEDING HELP:
- Elderly, disabled, intoxicated
- Don't put yourself at serious risk
- Use refuges where available

4. PREVENT RE-ENTRY:
- Stand at exits once outside
- Do not let anyone back in
- Reassure customers

5. LIAISE WITH FIRE SERVICE:
- Provide information on arrival
- Building layout, missing persons
- Access routes

Do NOT:
- Use lifts
- Go back for belongings
- Let customers return
- Fight fire unless trained and safe
- Put yourself at serious risk

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.3: MEDICAL EMERGENCIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Common Medical Issues at Venues:
- Collapse / unconsciousness
- Drug overdose
- Alcohol poisoning
- Allergic reactions
- Chest pain / heart attack
- Injuries from violence
- Falls
- Seizures

Response Steps:

1. ASSESS THE SITUATION:
- Is scene safe?
- What has happened?
- Is person responsive?

2. CLEAR SPACE:
- Move people back
- Give casualty space
- Dignity and privacy

3. GET HELP:
- Call for first aider
- Call 999 if serious
- Be prepared to assist

4. PROVIDE INFORMATION TO 999:
- Location (full address)
- What has happened
- Condition of person
- Age and gender
- Stay on line for instructions

5. ASSIST FIRST AIDER/PARAMEDICS:
- Clear access route
- Meet ambulance at entrance
- Direct to casualty
- Control crowd

Recovery Position:
- For unconscious but breathing casualty
- On their side
- Airway open
- Monitor breathing

CPR Basics:
- If not breathing normally
- 30 chest compressions
- 2 rescue breaths
- Repeat until help arrives
- Push hard and fast (centre of chest)

Specific Issues:

SUSPECTED OVERDOSE:
- Call 999 immediately
- Don't leave them alone
- Put in recovery position if unconscious
- Tell paramedics what you think they took
- Be honest - helping saves lives

GLASSING / SERIOUS BLEEDING:
- Call 999 immediately
- Apply pressure to wound (not on embedded glass)
- Do not remove embedded objects
- Keep casualty calm and still
- Preserve scene for police

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.4: SERIOUS VIOLENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Immediate Response:
- Ensure your own safety first
- Call for backup
- Call 999 if serious
- Separate parties if possible

Prioritising:
1. Safety of all persons
2. Medical attention for injured
3. Evidence preservation
4. Witness identification

After Violence:
- Preserve the scene
- Identify witnesses
- Secure CCTV
- Detailed documentation
- Cooperate with police

Weapons:
- If weapon used, treat as major incident
- Call 999 immediately
- Do not try to recover weapon
- Describe to police
- Preserve scene

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.5: TERRORIST INCIDENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RUN HIDE TELL (Review from Unit 1):

RUN:
- Escape if you can
- Insist others come with you
- Leave belongings
- Don't attempt to film

HIDE:
- If you cannot run, hide
- Lock/barricade doors
- Turn off lights
- Silence phone completely
- Stay quiet

TELL:
- Call 999 when safe
- What is happening
- Where
- Description of attackers

When Armed Police Arrive:
- Hands visible and empty
- Follow all instructions
- Don't run towards them
- Expect to be treated as potential threat initially

Door Supervisor Role:
- Follow RUN HIDE TELL
- Help customers escape/hide
- Provide information to police
- First aid if safe to do so

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.6: BOMB THREATS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If Bomb Threat Call Received:
- Keep caller talking
- Note exact words
- Ask: Where? When? What kind? Why?
- Note background sounds
- Report immediately to manager/police
- Do NOT use mobile phones near suspect area

Suspicious Package - HOT:
- H = Hidden?
- O = Obviously suspicious?
- T = Typical for this location?

Do NOT:
- Touch suspicious items
- Use radio/mobile near item
- Evacuate without authorisation
- Panic

Evacuation (if ordered):
- Follow management/police instructions
- Move people well away from building
- Different evacuation routes may be used
- Be aware of secondary devices

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.7: CROWD SURGE AND CRUSH
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Warning Signs:
- People unable to move freely
- Distress signals from crowd
- People being pushed
- Barriers flexing
- Crushing at entry points

Response:
- Open barriers/gates immediately
- Stop entry
- Do NOT push back against crowd
- Communicate calmly to crowd
- Call for backup
- May need to stop event

Prevention:
- Monitor capacity carefully
- Control entry rate
- Adequate barriers
- Multiple entry points

Aftermath:
- Medical attention for injured
- Evidence preservation
- Witness statements
- Incident documentation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.8: WORKING WITH EMERGENCY SERVICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Police:
- Provide information
- Control access to scene
- Preserve evidence
- Identify witnesses
- Follow instructions

Ambulance:
- Clear access route
- Meet at entrance and direct
- Provide information on casualty
- Assist with crowd control
- Give first aid until arrival

Fire Service:
- Assist with evacuation
- Provide building information
- Point out access routes
- Report any missing persons
- Follow their instructions

Key Principle:
- Emergency services have command
- Follow their instructions
- Support their work
- Don't obstruct

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 2.8.9: COMMUNICATION DURING EMERGENCIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Principles:
- Stay calm
- Clear and concise
- Use radio properly
- Don't block channels
- Face-to-face if radio fails

Radio Communication:
- Brief, clear messages
- Essential information only
- Avoid panic
- Confirm messages received

To Customers:
- Calm, authoritative tone
- Clear instructions
- Reassurance
- Repeat key messages
- Lead by example

Post-Emergency:
- Account for staff if possible
- Secure premises
- Preserve evidence
- Documentation
- Debrief

Question 1 [Easy]
What should a door supervisor do FIRST when hearing a fire alarm?

A) Find the source of the fire
B) Begin evacuating customers from their area
C) Call the fire service
D) Lock the main entrance

Correct Answer: B

Explanation: When a fire alarm sounds, the door supervisor should immediately 
begin evacuating customers from their area, directing them to the nearest 
safe exit. Finding the fire or fighting it should be left to trained 
personnel. Calling the fire service should be ensured but evacuation is the 
immediate priority.
