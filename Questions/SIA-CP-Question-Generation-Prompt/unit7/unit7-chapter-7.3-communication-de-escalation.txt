TOPIC 7.3.1: COMMUNICATION THEORY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Components of Communication:

THE 7-38-55 RULE (Mehrabian):
- Words: 7%
- Tone of voice: 38%
- Body language: 55%
- Non-verbal dominates

Verbal Communication:
- Actual words used
- Content of message
- Language choice
- Clarity

Para-Verbal Communication:
- Tone of voice
- Volume
- Pace
- Pitch

Non-Verbal Communication:
- Body language
- Facial expressions
- Eye contact
- Posture
- Gestures
- Personal space

Communication Process:

SENDER:
- Has message
- Encodes message
- Transmits

MESSAGE:
- The content
- Verbal and non-verbal
- Subject to interpretation

RECEIVER:
- Receives message
- Decodes message
- Interprets meaning

FEEDBACK:
- Response
- Confirms understanding
- Completes loop

Barriers to Communication:

PHYSICAL:
- Noise
- Distance
- Obstacles
- Environment

PSYCHOLOGICAL:
- Emotions
- Preconceptions
- Stress
- Fear

LANGUAGE:
- Different languages
- Jargon
- Accent
- Vocabulary

CULTURAL:
- Different norms
- Gestures meaning
- Personal space
- Eye contact expectations

TOPIC 7.3.2: VERBAL DE-ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Principles of De-escalation:

PURPOSE:
- Reduce tension
- Prevent escalation
- Avoid physical intervention
- Professional resolution

KEY PRINCIPLES:
- Stay calm
- Show respect
- Listen actively
- Be reasonable

Verbal Techniques:

TONE:
- Calm and measured
- Non-threatening
- Authoritative but not aggressive
- Consistent

WORDS:
- Simple language
- Clear instructions
- Non-inflammatory
- Respectful

PACE:
- Slower than normal
- Gives time to process
- Reduces urgency
- Calming effect

Active Listening:

WHAT IT IS:
- Full attention
- Understanding message
- Showing you're listening
- Responding appropriately

TECHNIQUES:
- Eye contact (appropriate)
- Nodding
- Brief verbal acknowledgments
- Paraphrasing
- Clarifying questions

Empathy:

SHOWING EMPATHY:
- Acknowledge feelings
- Show understanding
- Don't minimise
- Genuine concern

WHY IT WORKS:
- Person feels heard
- Reduces anger
- Builds rapport
- De-escalates

The Five-Step Appeal:

1. ASK (Politely):
   - Make polite request
   - Give opportunity to comply

2. SET CONTEXT (Explain):
   - Why you're asking
   - Reason behind request
   - Help them understand

3. OFFER OPTIONS (Choices):
   - Give alternatives
   - Let them choose
   - Maintain dignity

4. CONFIRM (Check):
   - Check understanding
   - Final opportunity
   - Clear consequences

5. ACT (Take action):
   - Follow through
   - If non-compliance
   - Proportionate response

Words to Avoid:

TRIGGER WORDS:
- "Calm down" (often escalates)
- "You need to..."
- "You always..."
- "Whatever"
- "I don't care"

INFLAMMATORY:
- Swearing
- Insults
- Sarcasm
- Dismissive language

Better Alternatives:
- "I understand..."
- "I can see that..."
- "Let's work this out..."
- "How can I help..."

TOPIC 7.3.3: NON-VERBAL DE-ESCALATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Body Language:

OPEN POSTURE:
- Not crossed arms
- Relaxed stance
- Non-threatening
- Approachable

POSITIONING:
- Bladed stance
- Not square on (confrontational)
- Creates space
- Ready but not aggressive

HANDS:
- Visible
- Open palms
- Above waist
- Not clenched

The "Fence":

WHAT IT IS:
- Hands raised, palms out
- Creates barrier
- Non-aggressive appearance
- Ready position

USES:
- Creating space
- Psychological barrier
- De-escalation tool
- Defense ready

Eye Contact:

APPROPRIATE:
- Shows attention
- Don't stare
- Avoid glaring
- Cultural awareness

TOO MUCH:
- Can be threatening
- Challenging
- Aggressive
- Cultural differences

Facial Expression:

NEUTRAL/CALM:
- Not aggressive
- Not smirking
- Shows control
- Professional

AVOID:
- Anger
- Contempt
- Fear
- Smirking

Personal Space:

REACTIONARY GAP:
- Safe distance
- Time to react
- Varies by situation
- At least arm's length

TOO CLOSE:
- Threatening
- Escalating
- Dangerous
- Unprofessional

TOPIC 7.3.4: UNDERSTANDING AGGRESSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Types of Aggression:

REACTIVE/EMOTIONAL:
- Response to trigger
- Impulsive
- Emotional
- Often de-escalatable

INSTRUMENTAL/PREDATORY:
- Calculated
- Goal-directed
- Controlled
- MORE DANGEROUS

Causes of Aggression:

INTERNAL:
- Mental health
- Personality
- Drugs/alcohol
- Frustration

EXTERNAL:
- Environment
- Provocation
- Crowds
- Time of day

SITUATIONAL:
- Perceived injustice
- Loss of face
- Lack of options
- Frustration

The Conflict Cycle:

1. TRIGGER:
   - Something starts it
   - Perceived threat
   - Provocation

2. ESCALATION:
   - Tension rises
   - Aggression increases
   - Can still de-escalate

3. CRISIS:
   - Peak of conflict
   - Most dangerous
   - Rational thought reduced

4. RECOVERY/PLATEAU:
   - Coming down
   - Still volatile
   - Careful management

5. POST-CRISIS:
   - Normal returns
   - May feel remorse
   - Opportunity to resolve

Intervention Points:
- Early is best
- Before crisis
- During recovery
- Not at peak (unless necessary)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
TOPIC 7.3.5: RECOGNISING PRE-ATTACK INDICATORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Why Recognition Matters:
- Early warning
- Time to respond
- Avoid surprise attack
- Better outcomes

Verbal Indicators:

WHAT TO LISTEN FOR:
- Threats
- Challenging language
- Refusing to respond
- Single word answers
- Personal insults
- Breathing changes (audible)

Physical Indicators:

FACE:
- Pale (blood to muscles)
- "Thousand yard stare"
- Target glancing (where they'll hit)
- Lips tightening
- Clenched jaw

BODY:
- Squaring up
- Shoulder drops
- Weight shift
- Clenched fists
- Removal of clothing/jewelry
- "Finger pointing"

BEHAVIOURAL:
- Invasion of personal space
- Refusing to comply
- Agitation increasing
- Looking for escape route
- Checking for witnesses

Target Glancing:
- Looking where they'll strike
- Repeated glances
- Chin, nose, groin
- Strong indicator

Response to Pre-Attack:

PREPARE:
- Adopt stance
- Increase distance
- Call for backup
- Final de-escalation attempt

CONSIDER:
- Pre-emptive action
- If attack imminent
- Based on honest belief
- Proportionate

TOPIC 7.3.6: MANAGING YOUR OWN STATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Self-Control:

WHY IMPORTANT:
- Professional response
- Better decisions
- De-escalation more effective
- Legal protection

CHALLENGES:
- Stress response
- Adrenaline
- Provocation
- Fear

Stress Response:

EFFECTS:
- Heart rate increases
- Adrenaline release
- Fine motor skill loss
- Tunnel vision
- Auditory exclusion

MANAGEMENT:
- Recognition
- Breathing techniques
- Training
- Experience

Tactical Breathing:

THE 4-4-4 TECHNIQUE:
- Breathe in for 4 seconds
- Hold for 4 seconds
- Breathe out for 4 seconds
- Repeat

BENEFITS:
- Calms physiological response
- Regains control
- Clears thinking
- Quick to do

Professional Detachment:

WHAT IT IS:
- Not taking it personally
- Emotional distance
- Focus on task
- Professional response

HOW TO ACHIEVE:
- "It's the situation, not me"
- Focus on outcome
- Training and practice
- Self-talk

Avoiding Escalation:

DON'T:
- Take things personally
- Get drawn into arguments
- Match aggression
- Rise to provocation

DO:
- Stay calm
- Maintain professional demeanor
- Focus on resolution
- Remember your role

Positive Self-Talk:
- "Stay calm"
- "I can handle this"
- "Focus on the job"
- "Professional"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Team Communication:

IMPORTANCE:
- Coordination
- Safety
- Information sharing
- Effective response

METHODS:
- Verbal
- Radio
- Signals
- Pre-arranged codes

During Incidents:

CLEAR:
- Concise messages
- Essential information
- Confirm understanding
- Update as needed

CONTENT:
- Location
- Number of people
- Nature of threat
- Assistance needed

Radio Protocol:

PROCEDURE:
- Identify yourself
- State message clearly
- Wait for acknowledgment
- Confirm critical information

TERMS:
- Roger (received)
- Wilco (will comply)
- Say again (repeat)
- Over (your turn)
- Out (finished)

Hand Signals:
- Pre-arranged
- Clear meaning
- Practice
- When radio not possible

Code Words:
- Discreet communication
- Pre-arranged meanings
- For sensitive situations
- Understood by team

Requesting Assistance:

WHEN:
- Situation escalating
- Outnumbered
- Weapons
- Need backup

HOW:
- Clear request
- Location
- Urgency
- Specific need
