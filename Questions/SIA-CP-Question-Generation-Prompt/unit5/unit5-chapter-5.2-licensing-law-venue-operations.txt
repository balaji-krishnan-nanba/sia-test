WHY THIS CHAPTER MATTERS
------------------------
Door supervisors must understand licensing law to ensure venue compliance
and avoid criminal liability. The Licensing Act 2003 governs alcohol and 
entertainment licensing in England and Wales. CPOs working door supervision
must know these laws and their enforcement.

TOPIC 5.2.1: THE LICENSING ACT 2003
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose of the Act:
- Regulates sale of alcohol
- Regulates regulated entertainment
- Regulates late night refreshment
- Replaced multiple previous laws

Licensable Activities:

SALE OF ALCOHOL:
- Sale by retail
- To be consumed on or off premises
- Licence required

REGULATED ENTERTAINMENT:
- Live music
- Recorded music
- Dancing
- Films
- Indoor sporting events
- Boxing/wrestling
- Some exemptions apply

LATE NIGHT REFRESHMENT:
- Hot food and drink
- Between 11pm and 5am
- To public
- Licence required

Four Licensing Objectives:

1. PREVENTION OF CRIME AND DISORDER:
   - Preventing alcohol-related crime
   - Drug prevention
   - Violent crime reduction
   - Anti-social behaviour

2. PUBLIC SAFETY:
   - Fire safety
   - Crowd safety
   - Building safety
   - Emergency procedures

3. PREVENTION OF PUBLIC NUISANCE:
   - Noise
   - Light pollution
   - Litter
   - Disturbance to neighbours

4. PROTECTION OF CHILDREN FROM HARM:
   - Underage sales prevention
   - Age verification
   - Child protection
   - Suitable environment

Door Supervisor Role:
- Supporting ALL four objectives
- Front-line enforcement
- Reporting breaches
- Professional conduct

TOPIC 5.2.2: TYPES OF LICENCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Premises Licence:

WHAT IT IS:
- Authorises premises for licensable activities
- Granted by local authority
- Includes conditions
- Displayed at premises

WHAT IT INCLUDES:
- Operating schedule
- Plans
- Hours of operation
- Conditions

WHO HOLDS IT:
- Usually premises owner
- Or business operator
- Named on licence

Personal Licence:

WHAT IT IS:
- Authorises individual to sell/authorise sale of alcohol
- Personal to individual
- Portable between premises
- 10-year duration

WHO NEEDS ONE:
- Those selling alcohol
- Those authorising sales
- Not all staff - just those responsible

REQUIREMENTS:
- Licensing qualification
- DBS check
- No relevant convictions
- Application to local authority

Designated Premises Supervisor (DPS):

WHAT IS DPS:
- Named personal licence holder
- Responsible for premises
- Day-to-day control
- On premises licence

RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Authorise alcohol sales
- Ensure compliance
- Staff training
- Named person

Door Supervisor Relevance:
- Work under DPS authority
- Support licence compliance
- Report to DPS/management
- Enforce conditions

Club Premises Certificate:
- For qualifying clubs
- Member clubs
- Different rules apply
- Similar principles

Temporary Event Notice (TEN):

WHAT IT IS:
- Permits temporary licensable activities
- Up to 499 people
- Limited duration
- Limited number per year

RELEVANCE:
- Special events
- May need door supervision
- Know what's authorised
- Conditions apply

TOPIC 5.2.3: LICENCE CONDITIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

What are Conditions:
- Requirements attached to licence
- Must be complied with
- Breach is offence
- Variable by premises

Types of Conditions:

MANDATORY CONDITIONS:
- Required by law
- All premises

- E.g., age verification

STANDARD CONDITIONS:
- Common conditions
- Local authority sets
- Crime prevention
- Public safety

SPECIFIC CONDITIONS:
- Tailored to premises
- Address specific risks
- May be added by review
- Premises-specific

Common Conditions:

DOOR SUPERVISION:
- SIA licensed required
- Minimum numbers
- Specific times
- Register kept

CCTV:
- Recording requirements
- Retention period
- Police access
- Quality standards

CAPACITY:
- Maximum numbers
- Counting systems
- Overcrowding prevention
- Fire safety

CHALLENGE 25/21:
- Age verification policy
- Challenge if appear under 25
- Acceptable ID
- Refusal if no ID

ID REQUIREMENTS:
- Passport
- Driving licence
- PASS card
- Military ID

DISPERSAL:
- Closing procedures
- Customer dispersal
- Noise management
- Taxi arrangements

Breach of Conditions:
- Criminal offence
- Fines
- Licence review
- Possible revocation

Door Supervisor Role:
- Know conditions
- Enforce conditions
- Report breaches
- Document issues

TOPIC 5.2.4: AGE VERIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Legal Requirements:

ALCOHOL SALES:
- Must be 18 to buy alcohol
- Must be 18 to be sold alcohol
- Offence to sell to under 18
- Offence to buy for under 18

PROXY SALES:
- Adults buying for minors
- Illegal
- Door staff awareness
- Intervention required

Age Verification Policies:

CHALLENGE 25:
- Challenge if appear under 25
- Request ID
- Refuse if no acceptable ID
- Industry standard

CHALLENGE 21:
- Similar but under 21
- Less common now
- Same principles

Acceptable ID:

FULL DOCUMENTS:
- Passport
- Full driving licence
- PASS accredited card
- Military ID card

NOT ACCEPTABLE:
- Provisional licence (some venues)
- Student cards
- Bank cards
- Unrecognised cards

Checking ID:

WHAT TO CHECK:
- Photograph matches holder
- Date of birth confirms age
- Document appears genuine
- Within validity date

SIGNS OF FAKE ID:
- Poor quality
- Wrong feel/weight
- Spelling errors
- Photo irregularities
- Security features missing

Refusal:

HOW TO REFUSE:
- Polite and professional
- Clear explanation
- Suggest alternatives
- No ID, no entry

RECORDING:
- Refusals log
- Details recorded
- Incident if escalates
- Evidence

Test Purchasing:
- Police/trading standards
- Young people used
- Testing compliance
- Failure consequences

Door Supervisor Role:
- First point of ID check
- Refuse if under 18/no ID
- Report suspicious documents
- Support venue compliance

TOPIC 5.2.5: ALCOHOL AND INTOXICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Legal Provisions:

DRUNK PERSONS:
- Offence to sell alcohol to drunk person
- Offence to allow drunk person to remain
- Door staff must refuse entry
- Must remove if inside

PROXY PURCHASES:
- Offence to buy alcohol for drunk person
- Door staff awareness
- Intervention if witnessed

Signs of Intoxication:

BEHAVIOURAL:
- Slurred speech
- Unsteady gait
- Loud/boisterous
- Impaired coordination
- Glazed eyes

PROGRESSIVE:
- Becomes more severe
- Judgment impaired
- Aggression possible
- Medical risk

EXTREME:
- Unconsciousness
- Vomiting
- Breathing issues
- Medical emergency

Refusing Entry:

GROUNDS:
- Already intoxicated
- Previous problems
- Likely to cause issues
- Venue policy

HOW TO REFUSE:
- Professional manner
- Clear reasons
- Don't argue
- Safety focus

Managing Intoxicated Persons:

INSIDE VENUE:
- Monitor behaviour
- Intervene early
- Suggest water
- Remove if necessary

EJECTION:
- Proportionate response
- Safe removal
- Duty of care continues
- Taxi/transport

WELFARE:
- Don't abandon
- Ensure safety
- Medical if needed
- Friends/taxi

Duty of Care:
- Extends beyond premises
- Vulnerable persons
- Safe departure
- Reasonable steps

TOPIC 5.2.6: DRUGS AWARENESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Legal Framework:

MISUSE OF DRUGS ACT 1971:
- Controls drug possession
- Controls supply
- Classification system
- Criminal offences

DRUG CLASSES:
- Class A (heroin, cocaine, ecstasy, LSD)
- Class B (cannabis, amphetamines)
- Class C (anabolic steroids, some prescription)
- Penalties vary by class

Venue Offences:

ALLOWING PREMISES TO BE USED:
- Offence if knowingly permit
- Drug use on premises
- Drug supply on premises
- Premises licence at risk

KNOWLEDGE REQUIREMENT:
- Must know or suspect
- Wilful blindness
- Active prevention required

Signs of Drug Use:

PHYSICAL SIGNS:
- Dilated/constricted pupils
- Excessive sweating
- Unusual energy/lethargy
- Sniffing/touching nose

BEHAVIOURAL SIGNS:
- Unusual behaviour
- Visits to toilet (frequent)
- Passing items
- Avoiding security

PARAPHERNALIA:
- Small bags
- Rolled notes
- Pipes
- Needles (rare in venues)

Drug Dealing Indicators:
- Short conversations
- Item exchanges
- Multiple contacts
- Specific locations
- Money handling

Door Supervisor Actions:

PREVENTION:
- Visible presence
- Random searches
- Toilet checks
- Dealing awareness

DETECTION:
- Observation
- Search if suspected
- Report to management
- Police notification

SEIZURE:
- Can seize suspected drugs
- Place in sealed container
- Hand to police
- Document everything

EJECTION:
- Remove from premises
- Report incident
- Document
- Police consideration

Drug Amnesty Boxes:
- Allow disposal without prosecution
- Voluntary surrender
- Reduces drugs in venue
- Police collection

Medical Emergencies:
- Drug reactions
- Overdose
- Call ambulance
- First aid if trained
- Don't ignore

Licence Review:

WHAT IS IT:

- Possible revocation

WHO CAN REQUEST:
- Responsible authorities
- Other persons
- Local residents
- Following incidents

OUTCOMES:

Offences and Penalties:

SELLING TO UNDER 18:
- £5,000 fine

- Possible closure

SELLING TO DRUNK PERSON:
- £1,000 fine
- Personal liability
- Licence implications

BREACH OF CONDITIONS:
- Up to £20,000 fine
- 6 months imprisonment
- Licence review
- Possible revocation

Closure Powers:

POLICE CLOSURE:
- Disorder or nuisance
- Up to 48 hours initially
- Extended by court
- Serious consequences

REVIEW FOLLOWING CLOSURE:
- Automatic review
- Within 28 days
- Possible revocation

Door Supervisor Implications:
